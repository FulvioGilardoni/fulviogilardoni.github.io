Fulvio Gilardoni is committed to making the Replay service accessible to as many users as possible, regardless of individual abilities, in compliance with Directive (EU) 2016/2102 and the European Accessibility Act (Directive EU 2019/882).

1. Reference standards

The Service is designed to comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at level AA, as required by the technical standard EN 301 549.

2. Measures taken

To ensure accessibility, we have adopted the following measures:

  • Use of semantic HTML and correct heading structure.
  • "Skip to content" link for keyboard navigation.
  • ARIA labels and role attributes for interactive components.
  • alt attributes on images and aria-label on icon buttons.
  • Full keyboard navigation support.
  • Color contrast compliant with WCAG 2.1 AA requirements.
  • Responsive design for mobile and desktop devices.

3. Non-accessible content

Despite our efforts, some areas of the Service may not be fully accessible:

  • Complex data tables with many columns may be difficult to navigate with screen readers.
  • Some third-party components (MudBlazor) may have accessibility limitations.

We are constantly working to identify and remove accessibility barriers.

4. Feedback and contact

If you experience accessibility difficulties when using Replay, please contact us:

  • Email: fulvio.gilardoni@gmail.com

We will do our best to provide the requested information in an accessible format and to resolve the reported issue.

5. Enforcement procedure

If your feedback does not receive a satisfactory response, you may contact the relevant national authority responsible for enforcing accessibility requirements in your country.
